# LinkedIn Post Draft

I completed a 3-day advanced EDA sprint on:

**E-Commerce Customer Analytics: RFM Segmentation, Cohort Retention & Market Basket Insights**

The goal was to move beyond basic charts and analyze customer behavior from a business decision-making perspective.

What I covered:

- Cleaned transactional e-commerce data using Pandas
- Built revenue, month, cohort, and customer-level features
- Analyzed monthly sales trends, top products, and order behavior
- Created RFM scores for customer segmentation
- Segmented customers into Champions, Loyal Customers, New Customers, About to Sleep, At Risk, and Lost Customers
- Built a cohort retention heatmap to understand repeat purchase behavior
- Identified frequently bought-together products for cross-selling opportunities
- Used Matplotlib, Seaborn, and Plotly for polished static and interactive visuals

Key learning:

EDA becomes much more valuable when charts are connected to business actions. Instead of only asking "what happened?", I focused on "what should the business do next?"

Example business questions answered:

- Which customer segments deserve retention campaigns?
- Which products can be bundled together?
- Which months need deeper sales investigation?
- Which cohorts show strong or weak retention?
- How can high-value customers be protected and grown?

Tools used:

Python, Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, Seaborn, Plotly, Jupyter Notebook

This project helped me practice advanced EDA, customer segmentation, cohort analysis, and business storytelling with data.
